SPELL LIMITATIONS

Spells learned by class. Swap spells only at non-combat or non-critical roleplay moments. Concentration: A caster may concentrate on one spell at a time. Maintaining concentration costs AP equal to the spell's activation cost each round.

ACTION POINT SYSTEM

Every character has a base 3 AP at the end of their turn. Various spells and abilities can increase this amount.

5 APLevel 6-9 Spells

After (LEVEL# - 2) uses in a single combat encounter, take 1d8 psychic damage per future advanced spell attack.

3 APCalled Shots

Attack with disadvantage to target a specific body part. On success, damage is resolved on a roleplay basis (e.g., disarming). An additional STR check may be required for extreme difficulty. Natural 20 causes permanent injury.

3 APSpells Level 3-5

All spells of levels 3-5 cost 3 AP to cast. After (LEVEL#) casts in a single combat encounter, take 1d4 psychic damage per future advanced spell attack.

3 APMulti-Attack

Class-specific multiple attacks with weapons.

2 APWeapon Attack

A single weapon attack.

2 APCantrips

First cantrip costs 2 AP. Each additional cantrip cast the same turn costs 1 AP.

2 APSpells Level 1-2

All spells levels 1-2 cost 2 AP to cast.

1 APMovement ¬∑ Disengage ¬∑ Interaction

INJURY & DEATH THROWS

Stabilizing Check (DC 10): Unlimited attempts (theoretically). Failures add to death throw count. Those proficient in Medicine have DC 5. Character rejoins fight with 1 HP and loses 1 AP for that encounter. Potions, abilities, and magic cannot heal until stabilized. Out-of-combat healing restores AP maximum.

Two Failed Death Throws: Receive one permanent injury (determined by roleplay or random chart). Many can be fixed through RP. Unlimited injuries possible.

Third Failed Roll: Roll a D20. If you roll a natural 20, you fall unconscious instead of die. A birthday 20 will get you back up to full health and fighting shape. If a 1 is rolled you die immediately, no heroic action can take place. Heroic Last Stand: You muster all the strength you have left. You may not flee the battle, as your last stand is here. You may not run to a healer, as your fate is sealed. You have 6 AP, and roleplay wise, you go out with a bang, making a significant impact in battle.

REST & RECOVERY

Long rests and short rests both restore full health. No mechanical difference.
